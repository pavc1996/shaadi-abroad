# Brand Identity — Shaadi Abroad

## Brand Overview

**Name:** Shaadi Abroad
**Tagline:** Your Dream Indian Wedding, Beyond Borders.
**Founded:** 2018
**Headquarters:** Toronto, Ontario, Canada
**Category:** Luxury Indian Destination Wedding Planning

---

## Brand Positioning

Shaadi Abroad occupies the intersection of **South Asian cultural expertise** and **world-class destination wedding excellence**. We are not a generic wedding planner who does Indian weddings. We are not a local South Asian wedding company trying to go international. We are the company that was born specifically for this: luxury Indian destination weddings, planned with cultural fluency, executed with white-glove precision.

**Target Audience:**
- Engaged Indian/South Asian couples aged 26–40, based in Canada
- First and second generation Canadians from Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and Sri Lankan backgrounds
- Predominantly Hindu, Sikh, and Muslim backgrounds
- Household income $150K+ CAD
- Live in: Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Edmonton, Ottawa, Brampton, Mississauga
- Often have family in India, UK, US, and Canada to coordinate

**What they want:**
- To have a wedding that honors their culture fully
- To not stress about logistics (they have demanding careers)
- To give their families an extraordinary, once-in-a-lifetime experience
- To look stunning in photos
- To actually enjoy their own wedding

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Voice:** Warm, sophisticated, confident, culturally fluent
**Tone:** Like your most accomplished, cultured friend who happens to plan weddings for a living

**Dos:**
- Speak to the emotional significance of the celebration
- Use culturally specific language naturally (baraat, mandap, sangeet, pheras)
- Be honest and direct about costs and timelines
- Celebrate the joy and complexity of South Asian wedding culture
- Use "we" — we're a team

**Don'ts:**
- Generic wedding industry clichés ("your special day")
- Overuse of exclamation marks
- Overpromise or use hyperbole
- Be formal or corporate
- Condescend or over-explain cultural traditions

**Example copy (good):**
"From the first dhol beat to the last biryani, your wedding should feel like you. We handle everything else."

**Example copy (bad):**
"We are excited to help you create your perfect special day filled with magical memories!!!"

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Ivory | #FDFAF6 | Primary background, clean and warm |
| Champagne | #F5E6C8 | Borders, dividers, subtle accents |
| Deep Maroon | #6B1A2A | Primary brand color, CTAs, headers |
| Muted Gold | #C9A84C | Accent, luxury signifier, highlights |
| Charcoal | #1C1C1E | Body text, dark sections |
| Soft Beige | #F0EAE0 | Section backgrounds, card fills |

**Color meaning:**
- Maroon — the color of Indian bridal wear, tradition, depth, celebration
- Gold — luxury, heritage, auspiciousness (mangalsutra, jewelry, marigolds)
- Ivory/Beige — clean, warm, modern, premium canvas
- Charcoal — sophistication, modern Indian editorial aesthetic

### Typography

**Primary (Headings):** Playfair Display
- Elegant serif, editorial, timeless
- Use for: H1, H2, section titles, quotes, brand name
- Weight: 400 (regular), 700 (bold)

**Secondary (Body):** Inter
- Clean sans-serif, highly readable, modern
- Use for: body text, navigation, labels, captions, CTAs
- Weight: 400, 500, 600, 700

**Type Scale:**
- Display: 60–80px (hero headlines)
- H1: 48–56px
- H2: 36–40px
- H3: 24–28px
- Body Large: 18px
- Body: 16px
- Small: 14px
- Caption/Label: 12px (often all-caps, tracked)

### Photography Direction

**Ideal images:**
- Multi-generational family moments at stunning venues
- Brides in vibrant lehengas against neutral/natural backdrops
- Intimate couple moments — not overly posed
- Ceremony details: marigolds, diyas, henna, jewelry
- Destination beauty: beaches, cliffs, villas
- Guests celebrating: dancing, laughing, emotional moments

**Avoid:**
- Overly saturated, HDR-style editing
- Generic stock photography
- Generic white Western wedding aesthetics
- Photos that could be from any brand

## Logo Direction

**Primary mark:** Wordmark
- "Shaadi Abroad" in Playfair Display
- "SHAADI" in maroon, "ABROAD" in gold
- Or full wordmark in charcoal for light backgrounds

**Monogram:** "SA" in stylized serif — use as favicon, embossed card detail

**Tagline lock-up:** "Your Dream Indian Wedding, Beyond Borders." in Inter Regular beneath wordmark

**Clear space:** Minimum 1x cap-height on all sides
